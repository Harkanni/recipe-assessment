A Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

A rich, smoky, and delicious West African rice dish. Perfect for parties, gatherings, or a simple comfort meal.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 15 mins
  • Cook Time: 45 mins
  • Servings: 4
  • Difficulty: Easy

Dish Image

Ingredients

Tomato Sauce

  1. 1 large, ripe tomato, cored and quartered
  2. 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
  3. 1 whole habanero pepper, stem removed
  4. 1 whole Fresno chili or jalapeño, stem removed
  5. 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  6. 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  7. 2 cups water

Rice

  1. 1/3 cup olive oil
  2. 1 red onion, diced
  3. 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  4. 1/4 cup tomato paste
  5. 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  6. 1 teaspoon curry powder
  7. 1 teaspoon cumin
  8. 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  9. 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  10. 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  11. 2 ¼ cups basmati rice
  12. 1 bay leaf
  13. 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste
  14. 1/4 cup chopped green onion (optional)
  15. 1/4 cup chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1

    Combine tomato, red bell pepper, habanero, Fresno chili, garlic, ginger, and water in a blender. Blend on high until smooth and set aside.

  2. Step 2

    Heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and salt and sauté until softened. Add tomato paste and cook 2–3 minutes, then add paprika, curry powder, cumin, thyme, pepper, and turmeric.

  3. Step 3

    Turn off heat and stir in the rice until coated. Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.

  4. Step 4

    Turn heat to high, add the tomato sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer. Cover tightly, reduce heat to medium-low, and cook for 20 minutes without opening the lid.

  5. Step 5

    Turn off heat and let rice sit for 12 minutes without lifting the lid.

  6. Step 6

    Remove lid and fluff rice with a fork. Garnish with green onions and cilantro if desired.

Tips

  • For a smoky flavor, allow the rice to slightly burn at the bottom.
  • Adjust heat level by modifying the number of chili peppers in the sauce.
  • If rice seems undercooked, pat it down gently, cover again, and cook on medium-low for 5 more minutes.
