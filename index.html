A Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

A rich, smoky, and delicious West African rice dish. Perfect for parties, gatherings, or a simple comfort meal.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 15 mins
  • Cook Time: 45 mins
  • Servings: 4
  • Difficulty: Easy

Dish Image

Ingredients

Tomato Sauce

  • 1 large, ripe tomato
  • 1 large red bell pepper
  • 1 whole habanero pepper
  • 1 Fresno chili or jalapeño
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 2 cups water

Rice

  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric
  • 2 ¼ cups basmati rice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp chicken bouillon paste
  • Chopped green onion (optional)
  • Chopped cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  1. Step 1: Blend peppers, tomato, garlic, ginger, and water until smooth.
  2. Step 2: Cook onions, salt, and tomato paste in oil. Add spices.
  3. Step 3: Stir in rice, add bay leaf and bouillon.
  4. Step 4: Add tomato sauce, simmer, then cook covered.
  5. Step 5: Let rest for 12 minutes.
  6. Step 6: Fluff and garnish.

Tips

  • Let the bottom slightly burn for smokiness.
  • Adjust peppers for heat.
  • If undercooked, cover and cook 5 more minutes.
